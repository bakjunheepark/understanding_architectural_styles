Can AI Understand Architectural Style?

We trained a deep learning model to classify 25 architectural styles, then tested whether it truly understands style by evaluating it on buildings that are understood to blend multiple styles.

Architectural style classification presents a uniquely challenging problem for machine learning. Unlike objects with clear boundaries, styles exist on a continuum. Buildings often combine elements from multiple traditions: a Gothic Revival church with Art Deco ornamentation, or a Beaux-Arts facade with Colonial proportions. Human experts frequently disagree on labels, and even the concept of "style" itself is contested among architectural historians. This complexity makes architectural style an ideal testbed for understanding what deep learning models actually learn: Do they memorize surface patterns, or develop meaningful representations of aesthetic principles?

Why This Matters

A reliable style classifier has many potential applications:

Architecture

Applying a client's stylistic preferences to select candidate prototype models

Urban Planning

Understanding the stylistic coherence of neighborhoods and streetscapes

Real Estate

Quantifying architectural character to study effects on property valuation

Heritage Preservation

Automated cataloging of buildings eligible for historic designation

We fine-tuned EfficientNetV2-L on the Xu et al. (2014) benchmark dataset of ~5,000 images across 25 styles, achieving 75.6% accuracy, matching prior state-of-the-art. But the real question isn't accuracy on single-label examples.

Human baseline: Dua et al. (2019) found that a trained architect achieved only 56% accuracy on style classification, highlighting that this task approaches the limits of human agreement. Many "errors" reflect genuine ambiguity.

The Real Test: Hybrid Architecture

To probe whether the model has learned compositional style features, we curated a novel evaluation dataset of 25 culturally significant buildings that architectural scholars explicitly document as fusing 2-3 styles. We then asked if the model can recognize these different styles.

Key Findings

What we learned from testing on hybrid architectural styles

🎯

Compositional Understanding

The model correctly identifies all styles in 32% of hybrid buildings, suggesting it has learned separable style features, not just holistic templates.

📊

High Partial Recognition

88% of buildings have at least one correct style in the top predictions, demonstrating robust style detection even in complex cases.

🔄

Human-Like Confusions

The model's errors mirror human expert disagreements: Georgian vs Colonial, Beaux-Arts vs Baroque, styles that share design vocabulary.

High-performing styles share distinctive visual signatures: Deconstructivism and Novelty have unmistakable forms, while Ancient Egyptian and Achaemenid benefit from unique iconography.

Challenging styles share design vocabulary with related traditions. Georgian and Colonial are both Anglo-American classical with symmetry and columns. Beaux-Arts and Baroque share ornate European monumentalism. Edwardian buildings are often misclassified as Georgian or Colonial, reflecting genuine ambiguity in British-influenced American architecture.

Overall Metrics Summary

Aggregated performance across all 25 hybrid buildings. 88% have at least one correct style identified; 32% capture all ground truth styles in top-k predictions.

Metric Definitions

All Correct (Top-k)
Percentage of buildings where all ground truth styles appear in the model's top-k predictions, where k equals the number of annotated styles for that building.
Any Correct (Top-k)
Percentage of buildings where at least one ground truth style appears in the top-k predictions.
% Styles Correct
Average fraction of ground truth styles correctly identified per building.
Mean Prob. Correct Styles
Average probability mass the model assigns to ground truth styles.
Mean Reciprocal Rank
Average of 1/rank for each correct style, measuring how highly the model ranks ground truth styles.
Jaccard Similarity
Set overlap between predicted and actual styles: |predicted ∩ actual| / |predicted ∪ actual|.

Per-Building Performance Ranking

Buildings ranked by percentage of correct styles identified. Note the wide variation in outcomes: from 100% on Cathedral of St. John the Divine to 0% on Azadi Tower.
